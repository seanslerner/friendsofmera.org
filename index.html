Who are the Friends of MERA?

We are a very concerned group of people including those who invested in MERA by way of donating money for the original purchase, by building an over 40-mile system of trails, and who regularly use MERA. Our rapidly growing group includes people with different backgrounds including some with expertise in forest management, soil science and forest ecology.

Friends of MERA:

  • Are committed to maintaining and developing the recreational values of the MERA.
  • Want to preserve the aesthetic and ecological values of MERA while supporting best practices to promote forest health, and,
  • Will promote awareness of and mitigate wildfire risk based on current science and forest management expertise.

In spite of strong objections from Friends of MERA and facts showing that the proposed logging and mastication method, and timing of logging would cause significant damage to the watershed and aesthetic values, Union County logged a ~50 acres area December 2021 through March 2022. As projected, the methods used to log and masticate, and timing of the project caused significant impacts to trails and the watershed (see document on the web page that was sent to Union County Commissioners on 5/10/10/22). Union County removed large fire-resistant pines and destroyed the natural beauty of the acre 50 area. Based on an email we received from Sean Chambers Union County on 4/28/22, the logging operation lost the County $39,796.00? “Project cost is estimated at $224,000.00 with revenues of $184,204.00 for a profit of $39,796.00.” (see Photos on website) Please refer to document on this web site: Mount Emily Recreation Area, Union County Oregon, Forest Stewardship plan, 2012. The purchasers of MERA clearly bought it to be managed as a recreation area using sound and scientific principles. MERA has been successful beyond what anyone thought. We would like to honor that as forest management proceeds.
